[Slide 16] Here’s one pass at explaining what philosophers do... Philosophers try to answer the really difficult and really important questions that science doesn’t seem equipped to answer . It’s worth noting, if you ask 10 philosophers to explain what philosophy is and what philosophers do, you’re likely to get 10 different answers. So, feel free to take this answer with an appropriate amount of reservation.

[Slide 17] The history of philosophy also supports this way of thinking about the discipline – especially the contrast with science. If you go back and look at ancient Greek philosophy, you’ll quickly notice that a lot of it is actually proto-physics. People like aristotle and Epicurus (who was one of the first to believe in atoms) were all trying to do physics by just, like, thinking really hard. Then, eventually, we realized that you can learn about physics better by doing science about it, by conducting experiments. Thus, physics splintered off from philosophy and became it’s own scientific discipline. (For the most part…) Philosophers no longer do physics from the armchair, and physicists are no longer doing philosophy.

[Slide 18] We see this same pattern in the history of Psychology. Until around the turn of the 20th century, most of the best psychologists were just philosophers: they were trying to figure out how the human mind works by just thinking about it really hard. But, again, we eventually realized that psychology can be studied scientifically – with experimentation and carefully manipulated real-world observation. Thus, psychology splintered off from philosophy. It became it’s own discipline. (For the most part…) philosophers are no longer doing psychology, and psychologists are no longer considered philosophers.

[Slide 19] Here’s one pass at explaining what philosophers do... Philosophers try to answer the really difficult and really important questions that science doesn’t seem equipped to answer . So, again, this is a helpful way of understanding the field. One benefit of this way of describing philosophy is that it makes clear that the boundary around what counts as philosophy may change as science progresses and become better-able to answer various sorts of questions.
