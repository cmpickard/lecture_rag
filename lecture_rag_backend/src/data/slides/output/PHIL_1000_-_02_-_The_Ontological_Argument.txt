SLIDE 20

[SLIDE BODY]
The Greatest Being God God is the greatest possible being – the being than which none greater can be conceived .

[PRESENTER NOTES]
Here’s the definition that we’ll be working with today. 3 points need to made here. By “great”, we mean “better” or “more admirable” or “more perfect” – God is the perfectest being. Also bear in mind that what’s relevant here is possibility, rather than actuality. So the class of things than which God is greater is all the possible things; all the things not in category 3, in other words. I cannot stress this enough: This is NOT THE ONLY POSSIBLE WAY OF UNDERSTANDING GOD. I am not claiming that this is THE definition of God. I am not claiming this is the best definition of God. Rather, this is how we’ll define God for the purposes of the Ontological Argument . This is what St. Anselm has in mind when he says ‘God’. When Anselm says ‘God exists’, he means ‘the greatest possible being exists.’ A s we’ll see in future lectrues, there are other good ways of defining God that are genuinely distinct from this definition. Other people might say ‘God exists’ and mean something like, ‘there was a powerful being who created our universe – that being exists.’

[SLIDE BODY]
Let’s define a great-making property as follows, a great-making property is such that when we consider adding this property to a being which lacks it, we invariably make that being greater.

[PRESENTER NOTES]
By “great” here, we mean something very generic like “more admirable” or “more valuable” or “improved” Thus, we could also describe a great-making property is a property that is always an improvement, always an upgrade when you add that property to beings which lack it. I know that’s a bit telescopic, so we’ll look at some examples.

[SLIDE BODY]
P1) God is either a Category 1 , a Category 2 , or a Category 3 entity. P2) God is not logically impossible C1) God is not a Category 3 entity (b/c we can imagine God!) P3) By definition, Category 2 beings don’t have the property of existence P4) Existence is a great-making property C2)	Thus, all Category 2 beings lack at least one great-making property P5) By definition, God is the greatest possible being C3) God is not a Category 2 entity (else he would lack a great-making property ) C4) So God must be a Category 1 entity (i.e. God actually exists. ) The Ontological Argument

[PRESENTER NOTES]
What happens next is exactly what you expect: Anselm argues that God is not in Category 2 either. On P3) to be in Cat 2 is to be a possible thing that could exist but doesn’t actually exist. Centaurs are Cat 2. They could exist but don’t actually. That’s true by definition of all Cat 2 beings. On P5): You can think of this as the claim the God is unimprovable : he’s the greatest possible being so he must already be at peak performance, perfect in every way, completely and utterly unimprovable.
