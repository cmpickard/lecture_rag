Seemingly, Elma’s belief doesn’t count as knowledge because she doesn’t have any evidence or justification. She’s just making shit up about history and, in this case, she happened to get lucky. Raul on the other hand, really has evidence. He knows his stuff.

Recall the “ Evidence Principle ” used in the Fine-tuning Argument . With small modification, we can turn it into a definition of evidence . A definition of ‘ evidence ’ An observation, O, is evidence for P over not-P (and is justification for a belief that P) when O is more likely or probable under P than under not-P.

[SLIDE BODY]
The Dream Hypothesis My senses seem to give me information about the world outside my body. That info is inaccurate when I’m asleep. Thus, if I’m not sure that I’m awake, I can’t be sure that my sensory info is accurate. I can’t ever be sure that I’m awake. Thus, I can’t ever be sure that my senses are providing me with accurate info.

[PRESENTER NOTES]
When I’m dreaming, I am prone to believe my senses – i.e. I confuse my dream experiences with experiences of reality. But of course, dream experiences do not represent an external reality . The things I dream aren’t the way I dream them to be; the events I dream do not actually happen. There is no sure way to tell whether I am dreaming and, if I am, for how long I’ve been dreaming.

[SLIDE BODY]
The Brain-in-a-vat Hypothesis My senses seem to give me information about the world outside my body. That info would be inaccurate if I were a brain in a vat, controlled by a supercomputer. Thus, if I can’t rule out the vat scenario, I can’t be sure my senses are accurate. I can’t ever rule out the vat scenario. Thus, I can’t be sure my senses are accurate.

[PRESENTER NOTES]
Here’s an updated version of the same scenario: Maybe when you were just 5 days old, some evil scientists removed your brain from your body. They placed your brain into a sophisticated Brain-Sustaining Vat and hooked you up to a very, very powerful supercomputer. Since then, the computer has been interacting with your brain constantly. It has been perfectly simulating for you all the experiences and brain states you would have had had you remained embodied. You’ve lived your whole life like this: interacting with simulated people, working at simulated jobs, perceiving a simulated world. The simulation tech is so good that there is no way to tell that this is what’s going on – no consistent and obvious signs that the things you’re experiencing aren’t real. This might seem surprising, given what you know about computers, but the world being simulated for you has less-sophisticated technology than the actual world. (i.e. the supercomputers in your version of the world aren’t powerful enough to do what the supercomputers in the actual world are actually doing to you .)
