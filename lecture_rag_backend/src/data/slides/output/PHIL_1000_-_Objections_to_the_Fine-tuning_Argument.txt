P1) Fine tuning : The laws of the universe could have been a little different, and if they had been, the universe would be uninhabitable P2) Evidence Principle : Take two competing hypotheses, H 1 and H 2 . An observation, O, is strong evidence for H 1 when O is far more likely under H 1 than under H 2 (i.e. when O is far more likely in an H 1 -world than an H 2 -world) P3) Fine-tuning is far more likely under H 1 (H 1 = God exists) than under H 2 (H 2 = God does not exist) C) So, fine-tuning is strong evidence for the existence of God. The Fine-tuning Argument

[PRESENTER NOTES]
And here’s the full argument. P1 is an observation from physics. P2 tells us how we should assess observations as evidence P3 tells us how this observation compares, given two different hypotheses – that God exists or doesn’t.

SLIDE 22
----------------------------------------
[SLIDE BODY]
Given that you’re alive, what are the chances you’d find alive yourself in Cleveland? If H1 is true? If H2 is true?

[PRESENTER NOTES]
If H1 is true: 100% – God can’t set you down anywhere but cleveland. So if God’s protected you, you’re basically guaranteed to find yourself in Cleveland If H2 is true: 100% – If God’s not protecting you, then again, there’s only one place where you can be alive: Cleveland. All living people are in Cleveland, so given that you’re alive, you must be too. So the observation that you’re in Cleveland isn’t evidence for either hypothesis. All living beings are in Cleveland; you exist, therefore you are in Cleveland. So you don’t learn anything new when you learn that you are in Cleveland, and you certainly don’t get important evidence that God is watching out for you.

Being in a fine-tuned universe is like discovering that you’re in Cleveland. Since only Cleveland supports life, all observers are in Cleveland. Since only fine-tuned universes support life, all observers are in fine-tuned universes. The Anthropic Principle Objection

[PRESENTER NOTES]
This is known as the anthropic principle: Since all possible observers exist in life-supporting universes, all observers will find that they live in a life-supporting universe.

SLIDE 27
----------------------------------------
[SLIDE BODY]
What’s our evidence? The universe we’re in supports life. OR There is a universe that supports life. This isn’t surprising. This is. The Anthropic Principle Objection

[PRESENTER NOTES]
The first claim isnt surprising. OF COURSE, the universe that we’re in supports life. We’re in it! We are life! Thus, our universe must support us. But that’s not the claim at issue. The second claim is the one that’s at issue in the Fine-tuning argument – and that seems genuinely surprising in a universe that happened by accident. What’s crazy is that there is any life at all – when there might very easily have been none. Given that there’s a universe that supports life, we would of course find ourselves there. But it’s still surprinsg that there’s a universe like that.

Suppose we live in a multiverse where each universe has different constants. Is it surprising that one supports life? That depends on how many there are! The Multiverse Objection

[PRESENTER NOTES]
With a bajillion universes, there’s a bajillion chances to get things just right. SO if there are lots of universes each with different physical law and constants, then its not surprising that one of them supports life. And it’s not surprising that we live in that universe. Thta’s like finding yourself teleported back home after a year of failed attempts.
