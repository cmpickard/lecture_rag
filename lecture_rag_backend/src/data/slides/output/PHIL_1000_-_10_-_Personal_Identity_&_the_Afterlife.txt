“You could never step into the same river twice” – Heraclitus

[PRESENTER NOTES]
All of this so far is, I take it, common sense. But that’s not to say it’s uncontroversial. The Presocratic philosopher Heraclitus famously thought that NOTHING ever survived a change – a view he famously encapsulated with this quote. (Here the thought is: by the time you go to step into the river a second time, everythings different: the banks have shifted a little, different waters swirl around your feet. And since everything’s different, it’s not really the same river. To change, he thought, is necessarily to be destroyed.

Non-physical souls either interact with matter, or they don’t.

[PRESENTER NOTES]
As soon as we start trying to imagine non-physical souls, we are confronted with a choice: We either grant that those souls interact with matter or we deny that they do.


SLIDE 36

[SLIDE BODY]
If there’s no interaction , then nothing in your soul ever changes anything in the physical world, and vice versa. That means (a) your soul doesn’t affect your body, and (b) you wouldn’t want it to. After all, your soul would have no information about what’s going on in your body. (no access to your emotions, sensations, impressions, etc.)

[PRESENTER NOTES]
Suppose we deny interaction. Clearly this isn’t a good way to go. If this is the view, then God, when She saves your soul, doesn’t actually save you. That’s because your soul doesn’t have any information about anything that ever happened in your life – it’s exactly the same as it was when you were born, unchanged by time and happenstance. It’s an empty ghost, indistinguishable from all of the other empty ghosts attached to all of the other people in the world.


SLIDE 37
----------------------------------------
[SLIDE BODY]
Alternatively, suppose souls do interact with our bodies. Since souls are non-physical, those must be non-physical interactions. From our perspective, that should look like uncaused brain events.

[PRESENTER NOTES]
Physical things interact physically with other physical things – and we basically understand those interactions. That’s what the entire discipline of physics is all about. We must now try to imagine a brand-new sort of interaction, a kind unstudied by physicists: a non-physical interaction between matter located in space and time on the one hand and immaterial, unlocated, non-physical soul-stuff on the other. Since our best science only has physical instruments with which to record and measure physical interactions, we must anticipate that these non-physical interactions will be, in themselves, undetectable. But although the interactions will be undetectable, the results won’t be. The results should look like uncaused events. If sould cause things to happen in the real world, then we should see their effects in the real world – even if we can’t directly measure the causation events. These effects should appear to us to be uncaused . And since souls are primarily thought to interact with our brains, we would expect, in particular, to observe uncaused brain events.

SLIDE 43
----------------------------------------
[SLIDE BODY]
Option #1: Jom acts like Tom. Option #2: Jom acts like Julius Caesar. If this happens, souls aren’t what matter for personal identity. If this happens, souls do matter – because they carry personal content .

[PRESENTER NOTES]
Option #1 just looks like a case where Tom survives the soul swap. That’s still Tom! Thus, in option #1 souls aren’t what matter. You can lose your soul and get a new one, but that doesn’t kill you, or change who you are. Seemingly, that’s because in Option #1, souls don’t have any personal content. There’s nothing about Tom in Tom’s soul, so nothing important is lost when his original soul is lost. In Option #2, it starts to look like Tom doesn’t survive. Putting Julius Caesar’s soul in Tom’s body means, i’m inclined to say, that the resulting person is now Julius Caesar. At least initially, that looks better for souls. Souls have some personal content, and so swapping souls means swapping persons.


SLIDE 44
----------------------------------------
[SLIDE BODY]
Does a person survive a soul-swap? Only if they don’t thereby lose their personal content. (e.g. memories, personality) But then… surely what matters is the persistence of that personal content , not the persistence of the soul.

[PRESENTER NOTES]
But there’s a problem. Think about why it’s plausible that Jom is Julius Caesar in option #2. What’s doing the heavy-lifting here? Why does Jom seem like Julius Caesar? Seemingly, it’s because he acts like and remembers being Julius Caesar. But then… surely what makes the person Julius Caeser is the fact that he has Julius’ memory/personality, rather than that he has Julius’ soul. On this view, then, it’s kinda confused to say that God needs to preserve your soul to bring you to heaven. The soul isn’t what matters; it’s just the vessel for the important stuff. Think of it this way: on Option 2 – when the resulting person is really just Julius Caesar reincarnated – the soul is no more and no less the cup that holds you. You aren’t essentially a cup; you’re essentially the content that the cup contains. And indeed, it seems like we could imagine God pouring you into a different cup. If She wanted, She could copy/paste your content into a new soul – or draw all your personal stuff out of your original soul and stuff it into a different one. For all we know, it could be that human souls are weak and crumbly like Dixie cups, so God is doing this soul-swap thing every night when we sleep! That would be ok though! The important bits would still be saved; you would be saved. We can tell because you wake up with the exact same memories and personality.

Since it’s possible for God to create cognitive duplicates of you (beings who have your memories/personality but aren’t you) , there must be more to you than just your personal content. (And it won’t work to simply say, “But God would never do that! She would wait until I’m dead to create a copy in heaven.” That might be true, but it’s beside the point. The point is that it’s not enough to simply create a duplicate of you in heaven. Since the duplicate isn’t you in the case where you’re still alive on Earth, surely it’s not you in the case where you’ve just died on earth.
