Reply Granted: maybe we can believe whatever we want about junk facts . But facts about religion aren’t junk facts !

[PRESENTER NOTES]
Recall, a junk fact is a fact that is so far removed from the circumstances of your life, at present, such that it seems clear to you that your beliefs about the fact will have no bearing on your behavior. But… That’s not true of religious beliefs. For pretty much everyone, what they believe about religion will absolutely have some impact or other on their behavior. It’ll affect what they do on Sundays, what they think about when they’re in pain, what they say to pollsters who ask about their religious beliefs, etc. Religious facts aren’t junk facts – they’re rather more important and impactful than facts about badger sightings 30 years ago in Ohio.
