[SLIDE BODY]
The Ontological Argument : Let’s examine the properties that define God. Surely we must conclude that a non-existent God is impossible. Thus, God exists. The Kalām Cosmological Argument: Consider the universe – a thing that is but might not have been. Surely the universe began to exist at some point, and that event requires an explanation. Why did that happen? Call the explanation, ‘God’ . The Fine-tuning Argument : Look at the universe. See how elegant and fragile it is? It seems very, very unlikely that such a thing could exist by accident. The best explanation is that the universe was carefully designed by God.

This is what the Evidence Principle is meant to capture. An observation is evidence for one hypothesis over another when there’s in difference in how surprising that observation would be under each hypothesis.

[PRESENTER NOTES]
The evidence principle says that the observation is evidence for whichever hypothesis makes the observation less surprising (if you like, you can think of the evidence principle as telling us to minimize the degree of mismatch between our evidence and our beliefs, or telling us to minimize surprise – we should adopt whatever beliefs are least surprising given our evidence

The Designer God God is the being that tailored our universe to support life

[PRESENTER NOTES]
What do we mean by “God” in the Fine-tuning Arg? Something like this: God is the being who fine-tuned the universe. It’s is absolutely imperative to note that this is not equivalent to the Greatest Possible Being God we talked about in conj w/ the ontological arg. The fine-tuning arg does not (and does not try to) prove that God is the greatest possible being. The fact the universe was fine-tuned does not show that God is unimprovable. It only shows that God has enough power to finely-tune a universe. Thus, the God we get from the Fine-Tuning ARgument is very similar to the God we get from the Cosmological Argument – it’s a Creator God. This being is responsible for causing the universe to be (and causing it to support life ) but beyond that, we cannot say much.
